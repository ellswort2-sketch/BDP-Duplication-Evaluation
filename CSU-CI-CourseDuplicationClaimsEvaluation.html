Review of CSU Channel Islands Duplication Concerns

Comparison of the proposed CSUCI Sustainable Agriculture B.S. and the proposed Coalinga College Agriculture Science and Technology B.S.

PSLO Analysis | Course Duplication Review | Core Requirements Review

Overview

The CSUCI Sustainable Agriculture proposal and the Coalinga College Agriculture Science and Technology proposal are both related to the broader field of agriculture. However, the programs appear to differ substantially in emphasis, technical instruction, and workforce preparation.

The CSUCI program description emphasizes sustainable agriculture, food systems, social responsibility, labor, policy, community engagement, and agroecological perspectives. The Coalinga College proposal is organized around agriculture science integration with technology, specifically precision agriculture, sensing systems, automation, AI/ML, remote sensing, robotics, and geospatial analytics.

Some overlap in agricultural subject matter is expected between agricultural degrees. The primary issue is whether the courses and programs are substantially equivalent in instructional purpose, technical content, and intended competencies to constitute duplication.

Course Comparisons

AGST 370 and SUAG 400

Coalinga College

AGST 370 Agriculture 4.0 Weed Management
AGST 370 integrates the foundational principles of weed management—biological, mechanical/physical, cultural, and chemical control methods—with emerging technologies transforming modern agriculture. Students will explore machine vision–based weed detection, AI/ML-driven decision systems, laser weeding, and autonomous spraying systems.

CSU Channel Islands

SUAG 400 Weed Management
This course provides an advanced exploration of weed management principles, including weed ecology, identification, and control strategies in agricultural and natural ecosystems. Students will examine integrated weed management techniques, herbicide modes of action, biological and cultural controls, and the economic and environmental impact of weeds. Emphasis will be placed on sustainable practices, regulatory considerations, and new technological advancements in weed control. By the end of the course, students will develop management plans that align with ecological sustainability and agricultural productivity.
CC CRPSCI 032 is our Weeds and Poisonous Plants class that aligns with SUAG 400. CRPSCI 032 is not a requirement for the CC degree. The focus of AGST 370 is specifically on technology and weed management integration. Thus, while both AGST 370 and SUAG 400 have elements of weed management, AGST 370 has a 21 units of upper division technology core prerequisite that is more narrowly focused on Agriculture 4.0 technology integration.

AGST 365 and BIOL 472

Coalinga College

AGST 365 Advanced Technologies for Integrated Pest Management
AGST 365 prepares students to integrate advanced agricultural technologies with Integrated Pest Management (IPM) principles to improve pest control effectiveness, economic performance, and environmental stewardship in modern agricultural systems. The course emphasizes the application of technologies (e.g., remote sensing, automated monitoring, autonomous systems, and data-driven decision tools) to support timely, site-specific pest management decisions. Students will learn to combine ecological and economic thresholds with emerging technologies such as unmanned aerial systems (UAS), machine vision, IoT-based pest detection, and machine learning–based pest pressure and crop growth stage models. Through applied case studies and project-based activities, students evaluate how technology-enabled IPM strategies can reduce chemical inputs, target interventions more precisely, minimize off-target environmental impacts while maintaining or increasing farm profitability, and support regulatory compliance.

CSU Channel Islands

BIOL 472 Integrated Pest Management
Integrated pest management is a comprehensive approach to monitoring and controlling agricultural pest in an environmentally acceptable manner. The ecological principles of pest management will be presented and practiced as they relate to plant pathogens, weeds and arthropod pests. The major strategies for controlling pests, including the use of natural predators, cultural practices and chemical applications, will be discussed. Students will also examine the current pest management practices of local agricultural systems.
Traditional IPM overlap exists. However, AGST 365 requires students to complete 21 units of upper division technology core that is more narrowly focused on Agriculture 4.0 technologies. AGST 365 adds remote sensing, automation, machine vision, and data-driven monitoring systems that were not identified in the CSUCI material.

AGST 380 and BIOL 452

Coalinga College

AGST 380 Technology-Enabled Agricultural Entomology
AGST 380 examines insects and mites of economic importance to Central Valley agriculture, integrating foundational entomology with precision pest management technologies. Students study pest identification, life cycles, host relationships, and ecological interactions, and apply this knowledge to modern IPM strategies, including the use of AI and machine learning models that integrate growing degree days, weather variables, and historical pest data to forecast pest pressure and optimize control timing. In addition to forecasting, the course explores technology-enabled spatial and automated pest monitoring systems (e.g., robotic and autonomous platforms that use machine vision and AI to map spatial pest distributions), remote sensing, and data-driven decision tools to guide management such as targeted and variable-rate control measures.

CSU Channel Islands

BIOL 452 Entomology
Explore the biology of the most successful and diverse group of organisms on Earth: insects. Insect morphology, physiology, ecology, behavior, taxonomy, and evolutionary history of the major insect orders will be discussed, as will the importance of insects to humans and biological control. Laboratories will emphasize collection, mounting and preservation techniques for insect specimens, as well as identification to the level of insect order and families.
The courses share entomology foundations. Howevewr, AGST 380 requires students to complete 21 units of upper division technology core that is more narrowly focused on Agriculture 4.0 technologies. AGST 380 extends those foundations into precision monitoring, AI-assisted forecasting, and digital pest management systems.

Conclusion

The CSUCI and CC degrees overlap in that they address the broader realm of agriculture education. However, the programs are substantially different in instructional direction and workforce preparation.

The CSUCI Sustainable Agriculture proposal is centered on sustainable agriculture systems, food systems, policy, social responsibility, and agroecological perspectives. The Coalinga College proposal is centered on integrating agricultural science with precision agriculture technologies, sensing systems, automation, remote sensing, robotics, AI/ML, and Agriculture 4.0 implementation.

At the course level, the CSUCI comparisons generally identify overlap in broad subject matter rather than equivalence in instructional content or technical competencies.
