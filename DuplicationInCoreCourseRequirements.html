CSUF Core Course Duplication Claims

Review of required CSUF Agricultural Systems Management core courses after excluding GE, transfer-level coursework, and electives.

Return to PSLO Duplication Analysis | Return to Course Duplication Analysis

Purpose. This page evaluates the claim of core-course duplication between the Coalinga College BS in Agriculture Science and Technology and the CSU Fresno BS in Agricultural Systems Management. The analysis focuses on the CSUF Ag Systems Management major core after excluding general education, community-college transfer-level coursework, and major electives.

Core-Course Duplication Calculation

The CSUF Agricultural Systems Management degree lists 65 major units. The duplication analysis should not count GE coursework, community-college transfer-level coursework, or CSUF major electives when evaluating duplication of required major-core coursework. After excluding those categories, 43 units of CSUF major core remain for the core-course comparison.

Of the remaining 43 CSUF core units, 31 units were not identified as duplicative in any way with Coalinga College BDP courses in the CSUF-submitted course matrix. The Course Duplication webpage (access link at top left of this page) examines the CSUF claims of course-level duplication and overlap between the CSUF and CC degrees. CSUF was concerned there was duplication between CC BDP curriculum and the following CSUF degree core courses IT 52, 116, 186, 194I, 196, and 199. The Course Duplication webpage notes that there is at most 30% overlap with IT 52, 0% overlap with IT 116, 100% overlap in the survey of GIS provided in IT 186 (54 hours of instruction) and a sequence of four required CC BDP courses (324 hours of instruction, CRPSCI 006, 008, 018, and AGST 325). There is broad overlap between AGST 345 and IT 194I and 196 in that each degree requires capstone work experience and a focus on certification. However, the certifications differ between degrees as would the focus of the work experience. The remaining core duplication claim is between AGST 385 and IT 199. Examining the course descriptions shows there is no duplication between these two courses. One is narrowly specified on-farm research trials requiring technology integration while the other is any approved problem or research project. This analysis indicates the only overlap in core course requirements is IT 186, a survey of GIS, and a 30% overlap with IT 52.

Evaluation

Core-course duplication:

The Course Duplication webpage (access link at top left of this page) examines the CSUF claims of course-level duplication and overlap between the CSUF and CC degrees. CSUF was concerned there was duplication between CC BDP curriculum and the following CSUF degree core courses IT 52, 116, 186, 194I, 196, and 199. The Course Duplication webpage notes that there is at most 30% overlap with IT 52, 0% overlap with IT 116, 100% overlap in the survey of GIS provided in IT 186 (54 hours of instruction) and a sequence of four required CC BDP courses (324 hours of instruction, CRPSCI 006, 008, 018, and AGST 325). There is broad overlap between AGST 345 and IT 194I and 196 in that each degree requires capstone work experience and a focus on certification. However, the certifications differ between degrees as would the focus of the work experience. The remaining core duplication claim is between AGST 385 and IT 199. Examining the course descriptions shows there is no duplication between these two courses. One is narrowly specified on-farm research trials requiring technology integration while the other is any approved problem or research project. This analysis indicates the only overlap in core course requirements is IT 186, a survey of GIS, and a 30% overlap with IT 52.

The main conclusion is that there is very little overlap and duplication between CSUF Ag Systems Management core courses and the required Coalinga College BDP core courses. In addition, topics in CSUF core courses not identified by CSUF as duplicative, includes accounting, statistics, agribusiness management, machinery, safety, electro-hydraulics, food safety, manufacturing processes, fluid power, quality assurance, and production operations.
