Evaluation of CSU Channel Islands Course Duplication Claims

Course-level comparison of selected CSU Channel Islands courses with proposed Coalinga College upper-division courses in the Agriculture Science and Technology B.S.

Overview

This document evaluates whether selected CSU Channel Islands courses are substantially equivalent to upper-division courses proposed within the Coalinga College Agriculture Science and Technology B.S. program. The analysis focuses on instructional purpose, technical specialization, prerequisite structure, expected competencies, embedded technologies, and the role of each course within the curriculum sequence.

Overlap in broad agricultural subject matter is expected among agricultural degree programs. The relevant question for duplication review is whether the courses are substantially equivalent in objectives, content, technical depth, and workforce preparation.

AGST 325 and ESRM 328

Coalinga College — AGST 325 Spatial Data Analysis and Decision Support Systems

Course Description: AGST 325 provides training and practice applying geospatial analysis methods and tools to support decision-making, thus equipping students with the skills necessary to integrate multi-source agricultural datasets into actionable decisions. Building on foundational spatial analysis, GPS, and GIS knowledge, the course emphasizes geostatistical analysis, multi-sensor data integration (soil, yield, weather, remote sensing), and the application of machine learning to generate field-scale management insights. (Prerequisites STAT C1000, CRPSCI 008, CRPSCI 018)

CSU Channel Islands — ESRM 328 The Why of Where: Foundations in GIS

Course Description: ESRM 328 introduces the principles and applications of Geographic Information Systems for environmental analysis and resource management. The course focuses on foundational GIS concepts and environmental/resource-management applications.

Duplication Evaluation: Both courses include GIS-related content, but the overlap is limited to broad geospatial concepts. AGST 325 is a specialized upper-division agricultural decision-support course requiring prior precision agriculture and GIS preparation. The course extends into agricultural geostatistics, machine-learning modeling, integration of yield, soil, weather, and remote-sensing datasets, and economic evaluation of precision agriculture strategies. ESRM 328 appears to function primarily as a foundational GIS course for environmental and resource-management applications. The courses are related in disciplinary area but are not substantially equivalent in technical scope, instructional purpose, or workforce competencies.

AGST 345 and BIOL 495

Coalinga College — AGST 345 Industry Certification and Internship

Course Description: AGST 345 is an integrated capstone that prepares students for professional success in the agricultural technology sector while providing supervised, real-world work experience. Students will identify and pursue industry-relevant certifications such as Pest Control Advisor Licensure (PCA), Certified Crop Advisor (CCA), and/or FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot. (Prerequisite 50 units of UD coursework and consent of faculty advisor)

CSU Channel Islands — BIOL 495 Agriculture Internship

Course Description: Supervised work and study for students interested in becoming Pest Control Advisers. Students will learn research and technical skills in relation to plant health, integrated pest management or production systems. May involve service learning. Weekly 1-hour seminar to discuss current issues in the agricultural industry. Repeatable by topic. Repeatable up to 9 units.

Duplication Evaluation: The courses share internship and professional-development components, but the instructional structure differs substantially. AGST 345 is positioned as an agricultural technology capstone integrating certification preparation, portfolio development, networking, and technical application of Agriculture 4.0 competencies in workplace environments. BIOL 495 focuses more specifically on supervised internship experience related to PCA preparation, plant health, IPM, and production systems. The overlap is in internship format and agricultural context, not in equivalent technical content or curricular objectives.

AGST 360 and BIOL 471 / SUAG 410

Coalinga College — AGST 360 Soil Fertility & Precision Nutrient Management

Course Description: AGST 360 integrates the principles of soil fertility with precision application technologies to maximize nutrient use efficiency by ensuring nutrient applications are delivered where and when needed. Principles include nutrient formulations, crop specific nutrient uptake dynamics, the roles and interactions of primary, secondary, and micronutrients, mechanisms of passive and active nutrient uptake, nutrient deficiency symptoms, and nutrient mobility in both soil and plants. These principles will be integrated with precision application technologies (e.g., remote and real-time crop and soil sensing, AI and ML nutrient demand forecasting, irrigation system optimization, pulsing, “spoon-feeding”, controlled release, and variable rate applications) to optimize crop production and resource efficiency. (Prerequisites CRPSCI 001 or 002 and SLSCI 021 and 21 units of Ag Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — BIOL 471 Soil Science

Course Description: BIOL 471 provides a fundamental understanding of soil systems, including soil organisms, microbe-plant interactions, nutrient cycling, soil fertility, and plant production. It addresses soil as a habitat and the role of soils in agriculture, environmental quality, and global environmental change.

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 410

Course Description: This course provides an in-depth exploration of plant nutrition, focusing on essential nutrients, their uptake mechanisms, and the physiological processes that support plant growth. Students will examine soil-plant interactions, nutrient cycling, and the impact of environmental and management factors on nutrient availability. Emphasis will be placed on sustainable fertilization strategies, integrated soil health management, and precision agriculture approaches. Through lectures, students will develop a deep understanding of theoretical models used to optimize plant nutrition in various cropping systems.

Duplication Evaluation: The courses share soil fertility and plant nutrition subject matter. However, AGST 360 is substantially more specialized in precision nutrient management and technology-enabled nutrient delivery systems. The Coalinga course integrates real-time sensing, AI/ML nutrient-demand forecasting, irrigation optimization, spoon-feeding strategies, controlled-release approaches, and variable-rate application technologies. BIOL 471 functions as a broader soil science course. SUAG 410 emphasizes plant nutrition and sustainable fertilization, but the available description does not demonstrate equivalent competencies in precision nutrient management systems, AI-assisted forecasting, or technology-enabled spatial nutrient optimization.

AGST 365 and BIOL 472

Coalinga College — AGST 365 Advanced Technologies for Integrated Pest Management

Course Description: AGST 365 prepares students to integrate advanced agricultural technologies with Integrated Pest Management (IPM) principles to improve pest control effectiveness, economic performance, and environmental stewardship in modern agricultural systems. The course emphasizes the application of technologies (e.g., remote sensing, automated monitoring, autonomous systems, and data-driven decision tools) to support timely, site-specific pest management decisions. Students will learn to combine ecological and economic thresholds with emerging technologies such as unmanned aerial systems (UAS), machine vision, IoT-based pest detection, and machine learning–based pest pressure and crop growth stage models. Through applied case studies and project-based activities, students evaluate how technology-enabled IPM strategies can reduce chemical inputs, target interventions more precisely, minimize off-target environmental impacts while maintaining or increasing farm profitability, and support regulatory compliance. (Prerequisites: CRPSCI 001 or CRPSCI 002; 21 units of Agricultural Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — BIOL 472 Integrated Pest Management

Course Description: Integrated pest management is a comprehensive approach to monitoring and controlling agricultural pest in an environmentally acceptable manner. The ecological principles of pest management will be presented and practiced as they relate to plant pathogens, weeds and arthropod pests. The major strategies for controlling pests, including the use of natural predators, cultural practices and chemical applications, will be discussed. Students will also examine the current pest management practices of local agricultural systems.

Duplication Evaluation: There is clear subject-matter overlap in IPM. The courses are not substantially equivalent because AGST 365 is framed around advanced technology-enabled IPM and quantitative evaluation of pest-control effectiveness, environmental risk reduction, and economic outcomes. BIOL 472 appears to be a more traditional IPM course emphasizing ecological principles and control strategies. AGST 365’s defining competencies are digital monitoring, autonomous systems, data-driven decision tools, and precision targeting.

AGST 370 and SUAG 400

Coalinga College — AGST 370 Agriculture 4.0 Weed Management

Course Description: AGST 370 integrates the foundational principles of weed management—biological, mechanical/physical, cultural, and chemical control methods—with emerging technologies transforming modern agriculture. Students will explore machine vision–based weed detection, AI/ML-driven decision systems, laser weeding, and autonomous spraying systems. (Prerequisite CRPSCI 001 or 002 and 21 units of Ag Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 400 Weed Management

Course Description: This course provides an advanced exploration of weed management principles, including weed ecology, identification, and control strategies in agricultural and natural ecosystems. Students will examine integrated weed management techniques, herbicide modes of action, biological and cultural controls, and the economic and environmental impact of weeds. Emphasis will be placed on sustainable practices, regulatory considerations, and new technological advancements in weed control. By the end of the course, students will develop management plans that align with ecological sustainability and agricultural productivity.

Duplication Evaluation: This comparison contains genuine overlap because both courses are weed-management courses. However, the focus differs. SUAG 400 appears to cover broad advanced weed-management principles across agricultural and natural ecosystems. AGST 370 is narrower and more technology-intensive, emphasizing Agriculture 4.0 weed-management systems, autonomous equipment, machine vision, AI/ML decisions, laser weeding, and variable-rate application. The overlap supports relatedness, not substantial duplication.

AGST 380 and BIOL 452

Coalinga College — AGST 380 Technology-Enabled Agricultural Entomology

Course Description: AGST 380 examines insects and mites of economic importance to Central Valley agriculture, integrating foundational entomology with precision pest management technologies. Students study pest identification, life cycles, host relationships, and ecological interactions, and apply this knowledge to modern IPM strategies, including the use of AI and machine learning models that integrate growing degree days, weather variables, and historical pest data to forecast pest pressure and optimize control timing. In addition to forecasting, the course explores technology-enabled spatial and automated pest monitoring systems (e.g., robotic and autonomous platforms that use machine vision and AI to map spatial pest distributions), remote sensing, and data-driven decision tools to guide management such as targeted and variable-rate control measures. (Prerequisite CRPSCI 001 or 002 and 21 units of Ag Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — BIOL 452 Entomology

Course Description: Explore the biology of the most successful and diverse group of organisms on Earth: insects. Insect morphology, physiology, ecology, behavior, taxonomy, and evolutionary history of the major insect orders will be discussed, as will the importance of insects to humans and biological control. Laboratories will emphasize collection, mounting and preservation techniques for insect specimens, as well as identification to the level of insect order and families.

Duplication Evaluation: BIOL 452 supplies foundational entomology. AGST 380 applies entomology to technology-enabled agricultural pest management. The duplication claim is weak because AGST 380’s central competencies are forecasting, spatial pest mapping, remote sensing, autonomous systems, and variable-rate pest management in Central Valley agricultural systems. BIOL 452 is broader biological entomology with laboratory identification and specimen techniques.

AGST 385 and SUAG 300

Coalinga College — AGST 385 On-Farm Research and Decision-Making

Course Description: AGST 385 trains students to design, implement, and analyze on-farm research trials that support data-driven crop management decisions in commercial agricultural systems. The course emphasizes scientifically sound experimental design under real-world farm constraints, enabling students to develop trials that maximize actionable information while minimizing cost, disruption, and risk. Students apply principles of experimental design and hypothesis testing to practical agronomic questions, integrating advanced technologies as appropriate, including AI and machine learning tools, decision support systems, remote sensing, IoT sensors, drones, and automated environmental monitoring, to efficiently collect and manage field data. Through applied case studies and project-based work, students learn to generate statistically defensible results that support economically and environmentally informed management decisions. (Prerequisite STATC 1000, SLSCI 021, CRPSCI 001 or 002, 21 units of Ag Technology Core, 21 and units of Ag Science and Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 300 Farming Practices

Course Description: In this experiential course, students will learn the principles and practices of farming. Topics include soil fertility, pest management, planting, harvesting, and irrigation. Concepts and skills will be applied in organic vegetables, berries, and/or fruit tree orchards depending on the season and farm needs. Harvested food will be donated to the campus community or sold at a farm stand/ farmer’s market.

Duplication Evaluation: The courses are not substantially equivalent. Even if SUAG 300 involves hands-on agricultural practice, AGST 385 is not a general farming-practices course. It is a research-methods and decision-making course focused on experimental design, statistically defensible field trials, sensor-enabled data collection, interpretation of agronomic and economic significance, and evidence-based recommendations. The overlap is applied agriculture, not course duplication.

AGST 390 and SUAG 470

Coalinga College — AGST 390 Technology-driven Regenerative Agriculture

Course Description: AGST 390 examines regenerative agriculture as a continuous, adaptive approach to enhancing soil health, biodiversity, and ecosystem resilience while maintaining or improving agricultural productivity and profitability. The course emphasizes achieving these objectives through the evaluation and integration of emerging agricultural technologies—including AI and machine learning models, remote sensing, IoT networks, drones, precision management systems, and autonomous equipment—to support data-driven regenerative management practices. (Prerequisite CRPSCI 001 or 002, SLSCI 021, and 21 units of Ag Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 470 Regenerative Agriculture

Course Description: This course examines the promises and pitfalls of regenerative agriculture as a climate solution. It discusses the numerous approaches to regenerative agriculture principles, practices, and outcomes through spotlighting farmers and initiatives that aim to increase climate resiliency by improving soil health and biodiversity. Students will learn about various regenerative agriculture certifications, government programs, and contemporary debates.

Duplication Evaluation: This comparison has direct topical overlap in regenerative agriculture. The courses are not substantially equivalent because AGST 390 is explicitly technology-driven and requires students to evaluate and integrate AI/ML, remote sensing, IoT networks, drones, precision management systems, autonomous equipment, and quantitative monitoring tools to support adaptive regenerative management. SUAG 470, as described, emphasizes regenerative agriculture principles, practices, certifications, programs, climate claims, and contemporary debates. The courses are related, but their instructional purposes and technical competencies are different.

AGST 395 and SUAG 370 / SUAG 410

Coalinga College — AGST 395 Smart Plant Health Management Systems

Course Description: AGST 395 examines the biology, ecology, and technology-enabled management of plant diseases within integrated plant health management systems. Students study pathogen biology, disease cycles, and host–pathogen interactions and apply this knowledge using advanced monitoring and decision-support technologies. The course emphasizes the use of remote sensing, UAS-based imaging, IoT-enabled spore and disease monitoring, and AI/ML-powered detection tools to support early diagnosis, spatial disease assessment, and targeted management interventions. (Prerequisite CRPSCI 001 or 002 and 21 units of Ag Technology Core)

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 370 Smart Agriculture

Course Description: This course explores the transformative role of modern technologies in agriculture to promote sustainability, efficiency, and food security. Students will gain a high-level understanding of tools such as precision agriculture, remote sensing, IoT (Internet of Things), AI, robotics, and data analytics, and how these are integrated into real-world agricultural systems. The course emphasizes critical thinking about ethical, environmental, and social impacts of technological change in agriculture, while remaining accessible to students without a technical background.

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 410

Course Description: This course provides an in-depth exploration of plant nutrition, focusing on essential nutrients, their uptake mechanisms, and the physiological processes that support plant growth. Students will examine soil-plant interactions, nutrient cycling, and the impact of environmental and management factors on nutrient availability. Emphasis will be placed on sustainable fertilization strategies, integrated soil health management, and precision agriculture approaches. Through lectures, students will develop a deep understanding of theoretical models used to optimize plant nutrition in various cropping systems.

Duplication Evaluation: The duplication claim is weak unless SUAG 370 or SUAG 410 includes substantial plant disease diagnostics, pathogen biology, AI/ML-based disease detection, remote sensing, spore monitoring, disease-risk modeling, spatial disease mapping, and autonomous or variable-rate disease-management systems. AGST 395 is specifically a smart plant-health systems course. SUAG 370 is a broad smart agriculture course designed to provide high-level exposure to modern agricultural technologies and their ethical, environmental, and social implications. SUAG 410 is focused on plant nutrition. Neither description establishes equivalence to AGST 395’s disease-centered plant health systems content and technology-enabled diagnosis, prediction, and targeted intervention competencies.

ECON 360 and SUAG 370

Coalinga College — ECON 360 Precision Agriculture Economics and Technology Adoption

Course Description: ECON 360 integrates economic analysis, market evaluation, and strategic planning for precision agriculture technology adoption in California's Central Valley. Students will learn to conduct cost-benefit analyses, assess ROI, and evaluate scalability for technologies ranging from automated machinery to IoT-based sensing systems. (Prerequisite AGBUS 040)

CSU Channel Islands — SUAG 370 Smart Agriculture

Course Description: This course explores the transformative role of modern technologies in agriculture to promote sustainability, efficiency, and food security. Students will gain a high-level understanding of tools such as precision agriculture, remote sensing, IoT (Internet of Things), AI, robotics, and data analytics, and how these are integrated into real-world agricultural systems. The course emphasizes critical thinking about ethical, environmental, and social impacts of technological change in agriculture, while remaining accessible to students without a technical background.

Duplication Evaluation: This is not a strong duplication comparison. ECON 360 is an economics, adoption, and strategic planning course. Its defining competencies are cost-benefit analysis, ROI assessment, scalability evaluation, market and policy analysis, and development of economically justified technology adoption plans for agricultural operations. SUAG 370 is a broad smart agriculture overview course that introduces precision agriculture, remote sensing, IoT, AI, robotics, and data analytics while emphasizing ethical, environmental, and social impacts. The courses may refer to similar agricultural technologies, but they do so for different instructional purposes. Shared reference to technology does not make an economics and adoption-planning course substantially equivalent to a broad smart agriculture survey course.

Conclusion

The reviewed CSUCI and Coalinga College courses frequently occupy related agricultural subject areas. However, the Coalinga College Agriculture Science and Technology curriculum consistently integrates advanced Agriculture 4.0 implementation, including sensors, GIS and geostatistics, AI/ML, IoT systems, remote sensing, autonomous systems, predictive analytics, variable-rate technologies, and decision-support workflows.

The CSUCI courses generally emphasize sustainable agriculture, biological systems, environmental science, agricultural practice, and ecological principles. The Coalinga College courses are more narrowly focused on technology-integrated agricultural implementation and advanced precision agriculture competencies. Based on the available descriptions, the comparisons support findings of disciplinary relatedness and partial topical overlap rather than substantial duplication.
