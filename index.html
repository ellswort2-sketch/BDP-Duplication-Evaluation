Purpose: Curriculum mapping is used here to evaluate the claimed duplication between PSLOs. For each claim, the courses mapped as I = Introduced and E = Emphasized are shown side by side. Select a course title to expand or collapse its description.

First Comparison

CC PSLO 1 ↔ CSUF PSLO A4

CC PSLO 1

Technology-Integrated Agricultural Systems: Implement, maintain, and troubleshoot agricultural technology systems that integrate sensors, PLCs, SCADA platforms, and/or CAN Bus/ISOBUS protocols with agronomic science principles to optimize operational efficiency, resource management, and regulatory compliance.
EEmphasized Courses Mapped to Program Learning Outcome
AGST 305Ag Sensors, Networks, and Data Acquisition Systems
AGST 305 provides training in the deployment, calibration, and validation of agricultural sensing and data acquisition systems that support modern agriculture. The course emphasizes sensor measurement principles, environmental influences on sensor performance, signal conditioning, and data reliability in agricultural field environments. Students gain applied experience with IoT architectures and wireless communication protocols commonly used in agriculture, including LoRaWAN and Zigbee, to transmit sensor data from the field to control or decision-support platforms.
AGST 310Agricultural Control Systems
AGST 310 provides instruction in the design, implementation, and optimization of integrated agricultural control systems used in crop production, irrigation management, harvesting operations, and food and fiber processing. Students apply process control concepts, PLC programming, SCADA systems, and machine communication networks to coordinate agricultural operations at the system level. Students work with CAN Bus and ISOBUS networks and address regulatory compliance, safety, and operational efficiency.
AGST 340Agricultural Technology Project Planning & Implementation
AGST 340 applies formal project management principles to the planning and implementation of agricultural technology systems in real-world production environments. Students translate technical requirements from automation, sensing, and control systems into project plans that account for seasonal constraints, weather variability, regulatory considerations, and operational risk.
IIntroduced Courses Mapped to Program Learning Outcome
AET 005Fundamentals of Electronics (DC)
AET 05 provides an introduction to the fundamental concepts of electricity and electronics. Students will explore the basics of electrical components, tools, measurement devices, and direct current (DC) circuits. (CSU)
CRPSCI 017Control and Sensor Systems in Ag
CRPSCI 017 provides students with concepts of sensors and control systems. Fundamentals of GPS, GIS, telemetry, hydraulics, pneumatics, electronics and programming are covered as underlying technologies. The second portion of the course applies these technologies to auto guidance, variable rate, autonomous UAS, field sensors, fertigation and irrigation control systems, and livestock sensors. Hands-on activities include installation and use of these sensor and control systems.
AGST 375Climate-Smart Irrigation for Agriculture
AGST 375 provides instruction in modern irrigation technologies to improve water-use efficiency and system performance in water-limited agricultural systems, with emphasis on California’s Central Valley and SGMA constraints. Technologies include VFD pumping systems, automated irrigation control and monitoring platforms, plant- and soil-based water stress sensors, and remote sensing of crop water status.
SOC 370Agricultural Technology Law, Ethics, and Cybersecurity
SOC 370 covers legal, ethical, and regulatory issues related to Agriculture 4.0 technology including data privacy, environmental regulations, worker safety, professional ethics, cybersecurity principles for agricultural systems, data governance, secure IoT implementation, sensor spoofing, cyber threats, and blockchain validation systems.

CSUF PSLO A4

Be able to apply scientific and mathematical principles and practices to agricultural and industrial applications.
EEmphasized Courses Mapped to Program Learning Outcome
AGBS 71Agricultural Business Statistics
Study of statistical techniques and formal reasoning applications to management and social and agricultural sciences. Calculation, interpretation, critical evaluation, and historical relevance of quantitative tools, data analysis, and results including graphical presentations, descriptive and inferential statistics.
MATH 11Elementary Statistics
Illustration of statistical concepts: elementary probability models, sampling, descriptive measures, confidence intervals, testing hypotheses, chi-square, nonparametric methods, and regression.
IT 117Quality Assurance
Quality assurance principles and practices in industry: quality assurance systems, acceptance sampling, testing, source surveillance; probability and statistical concepts, process control techniques and measurement procedures as applied to quality.
IIntroduced Courses Mapped to Program Learning Outcome

No courses identified in this category.

Mapping evaluation: This comparison shows limited alignment. CC PSLO 1 is centered on applied agricultural technology systems, including sensors, PLCs, SCADA, CAN Bus/ISOBUS, diagnostics, and field implementation. CSUF A4 is centered on scientific, mathematical, statistical, and quality-analysis applications.
